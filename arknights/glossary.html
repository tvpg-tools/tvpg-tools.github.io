Glossary

Aerial New

Enemies that can fly over the map, ignoring its topography, have this designation. May sometimes be described as a "Flying unit."

Arts Resist (RES) New

The percentage amount of arts damage that can be mitigated by an operator.

Attack (ATK) New

The maximum amount of damage the operator can deal when attacking.

Attributes

The offensive and defensive capabilities of an operator. Includes:

  • Hit points (HP)
  • Attack (ATK)
  • Defense (DEF)
  • Arts Resist (RES)
  • Redeploy
  • Cost
  • Block
  • Attack Speed (ASPD)
Battle Record

An item used to improve your operators. Has four tiers:

  • Drill Battle Record: Provides 200 EXP
  • Frontline Battle Record: Provides 400 EXP
  • Tactical Battle Record: Provides 1000 EXP
  • Strategic Battle Record: Provides 2000 EXP
Block New

Describes the maximum number of enemy units the operator can intercept. Some enemy units may "consume" or require more than 1 block in order to be intercepted.

Branch

The sub-class that an operator belongs to. Determines the operator's Trait.

Cost New

The amount of deployment points required to deploy an operator. Increases after the first deployment.

Damage

The minimum amount of damage done is 5% of ATK. Separated into three types:

  • Physical: Mitigated at a flat rate by a character's DEF stat
  • Arts: Mitigated at a percentage rate based on character's RES stat
  • True: Ignores DEF and RES; deals damage equal to the character's ATK
Damage per Second (DPS)

A metric used in determining an operator's "strength," typically against a single target. Can also be used to refer to characters who are damage focused.

Defense (DEF) New

The amount of physical damage that an operator can mitigate when attacked.

Deployment Points (DP)

Required to deploy operators. Vanguard operators can generate DP either by using their skills or by defeating enemies.

Headhunting

A method of acquiring new operators that requires orundums or Headhunting Permits. Players have a better chance of recruiting higher-rarity operators than through recruitment.

Headhunting Permit

A special item that can be used for Headhunting in lieu of Orundums. Comes in single- and ten-roll variants.

Hit Points (HP) New

The amount of damage an operator can take before being forcibly retreated.

HP Seal New

The maximum number of times enemies can enter the Objective before you fail the operation. The number of HP Seals available is found at the top of the screen during the operation.

Job

The categories into which all operators are separated. Jobs are sub-divided into Branches.

Lane New

A path that enemy units will take to reach the objective.

Leak New

When an enemy breaks through your defense, and potentially enters the Objective.

Melee

Melee operators are those who can be placed on "low" tiles, and typically engage the enemy via blocking. Melee enemies can only attack operators that they are blocked by.

Originite Prime

A premium currency that can be used to purchase skins for characters, refresh sanity, converted into orundums, or recharging drones in the base.

Orundum

A currency required for Headhunting. Can be acquired by completing missions and Annihilation stages.

Lungmen Dollars (LMD)

A resource required for improving operators and producing some materials in the Base.

Ranged

Ranged operators are those that can be placed on "high" tiles. Ranged operators will generally focus the enemies in range that currently have the shortest path to their targeted Objective. Marksmen Snipers, for example, will prioritize Aerial enemies, even if there is a melee enemy (in range) about to enter the Objective.
Ranged enemies will attack the most recently deployed operator within their range, unless otherwise specified.

Recruiting

A means of acquiring new operators that requires Recruitment Permits and LMD. Tags can be used to increase the chance of acquiring certain operators.

Redeploy New

The relative amount of time before an operator can be redeployed after retreating.

Sanity

The resource required to attempt most stages. Regenerates at a rate of 1 every 6 minutes.

Skill

A special ability that can be used by an operator. Most skills require SP to activate. There are three ways in which skills are activated:

  • Auto: Activates immediately upon the requisite number of SP being acquired.
  • Manual: Activated at the player's discretion once enough SP have been acquired.
  • Passive: Activates when the operator is deployed.
Skill Point (SP)

May be required to activate an operator's skill, and can be generated in one of three ways:

  • Auto Gained at a rate of 1 per second.
  • Offensive Gained every time the operator attacks.
  • Defensive Gained every time the operator is attacked.
Skill Summary

Used to improve operators' skills in combination with other materials. Has three tiers:

  • Skill Summary - 1: Used to improve skills up to rank 3.
  • Skill Summary - 2: Used to improve skills up to rank 6.
  • Skill Summary - 3: Used to improve skills to rank 7 and for Skill Mastery.
Talent

A bonus that is generally unique to a given operator. It may also provide bonuses to other operators.

Trait

The "defining characteristic" of the operator as defined by their Branch.

Trust

Gained by using operators to complete stages. Operators will gain trust experience equal to the amount of Sanity consumed. Trust provides stat bonuses to characters and provides the maximum bonus at 100 Trust. However, when characters are set as Support Units the maximum bonus is only provided at 200 Trust. It also unlocks further information under their profile and is a requirement for unlocking the operator's Module.
